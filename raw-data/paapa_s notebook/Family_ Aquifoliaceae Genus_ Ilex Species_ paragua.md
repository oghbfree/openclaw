Family: Aquifoliaceae     Genus: Ilex     Species: paraguariensis

Ilex Paraguariensis (Yerba Mate)is a species of holly. This plant is native to Argentina. It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow to 20 m high in the wild. Commonly, when cultivated, it is pruned into a shrubby, 4-8 m tall tree to make harvesting easier.

Advice on how to grow Yerba

Mate is happiest in a slightly acid (pH 7.2),sandy fine-grained, fairly deep soil with good drainage. The soil should be partially clay-like or sandy, well-draining and rich in iron and phosphoric potassium.

Plant your maté seeds a centimetre or two below the soil's surface and press down on the soil to hold them in place. Seeds need to be stratified (layers of moist sand  **which means putting down layers of sand and soil to plant the seed in**\sterile medium (milled sphagnum moss and vermiculite) and may take between two months and a year to germinate.  It is relatively difficult to secure rooting on a shoot/ seedling. Yerba mate seeds have to be sewn immediately or they must be pre-treated by stratification, otherwise they will lose the ability to germinate. **Soak seeds for 24 hours in your favourite B1 growth hormone (we use thrive alive organic available from Technaflora plant products but any similar product will work).** The relatively short life of the seeds and the low ability to germinate makes propagation of yerba mate quite problematic.

Light: Full or partial sun; a southern, eastern, or western exposure.Growing in full sun and keeping temperatures for the most partabove 60 degrees will increase leaf production.

Humidity: Preferably above 50% moderate to high; however, they can tolerate lower levels without any difficulty. Although it can stand temperatures as low as -8 it should never dip lower then this. It can only tolerate this cold for a short time.

Natural Climate: Rainfall averages about 65 inches per year, and temperatures are between 60 and 85 degrees F. 16-25 degrees Celsius

**Watering: Bring the soil to visual dryness between waterings and then thoroughly saturate.** (To avoid daily watering daily place pine chips on the surface to avoid evaporation of water at no time should there be free standing water!)

**Fertilizer: Moderate feeders. Apply a balanced fertilizer like a 15-15-15 or a 7-9-5weekly or biweekly from spring to fall.**

Pruning: When plants are young, pinching the tips of the expanding growth willhelp create a dense specimen and increase leaf volume. They do tend tohave an outreaching growth habit which pruning and pinching controls.

Insects andDisease:We see few if any problems with insects or diseases. Scale and mealy

bugs can attack plants if exposed to other infected plants. Bugs love to eat the Maté leaves, but no pesticides are ever used to control this; instead the bugs are removed by hand as the indigenous people walk along the plants and remove them one by one.Weed control is by hand by the use of large machetes.

Cultivation: After planting out, the young mate tree takes at least 2 years to establish itself.  TOnce the plant has been established and has 4-5 leaves you may now transplant it to its new home. Place your plant in partial shade as if it were under cover of the rain forest It should remain in this location for the next 9-12 months after which you may transplant it into the ground for those in the South or into a Large pot which can be wintered indoors for those in the North. Harvesting can begin as early as 3 years after planting though sometimes conditions require waiting up to 5 years.
