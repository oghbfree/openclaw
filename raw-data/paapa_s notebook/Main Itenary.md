As a teacher, I have worked with Children who are autistic. I will not sugar coat it. These children can test you. They see and feel things differently than other children. They can be overstimulated by too much input, too many sounds, too many changes to routine, too much visual stimuli. When they are on overload you will see the type of behavior you talked about. Some of the best ways I help the children I have worked with is teaching them ways to control and manage their behaviors when they are moving into overload. Teaching deep breathing helps as well as the words that will tell you he "needs a time aside" (not time out). He told you in the video he needed a break so he is halfway there to understanding how to control things. When he loses control it is best to stop what you are doing and simply say "you are not in control" leave where you are and find a quiet place where he can freely and quietly gain control. Often this time aside is a time when he can practice deep breathing, jump up and down or press on a wall or floor to release the tension. Once he is back in control you can help him understand what bothered him and how he can react in the future. Sometimes these children process social information slower than others so don't use too much language or expecting quick responses to directions. It just will not work. Instead, try using words that will give him time to react. In a while we will.... is a cue to let him now that something is changing. If he does not follow a direction you could say "we will decide/ talk about this again/ later. A firm "you are not in control" tells him he needs a time aside to regain control.

Above all, you are so right to try and spend more time with him. I have work with several children who are now doing well in school and have a network of friends that understand them. The children I have worked with have a gift of seeing the world in a way that is very special. We just need to slow down and respect how they learn and see the world. Good luck and God Bless. Your heart and love will see you and him through this.
