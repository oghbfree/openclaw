Yerba, Mind & Life

Description:

Yerba Mate is a 100% natural tea harvested exclusively from the Ilex Paraguariensis tree, which grows in South America.

Once the leaves and stems are harvested they are dried and chopped up, then matured for 12-24 months before being packaged. Yerba mate tea is also known simply as mate, pronounced, "matte" and is drunk mainly in Argentina, Uruguay, Paraguay and Brazil.

Today all people: young, old, rich and poor drink mate daily.

In these countries one doesn't meet with someone for coffee, you meet for a mate. Yerba mate is naturally caffeinated containing about half the caffeine of coffee and is said to give you a boost without the jitters. Yerba mate also contains more antioxidants than green tea and about half of your RDA percentage of vitamin B.

Also, do not be intimidated by the pictures which are on this site. You do not have to know how to prepare Yerba Mate the way they brew it in South America, with a gourd and bombilla, in order to experience it.

Yerba Mate in tea bag form is available and a convenient way to enjoy yerba mate. The taste and health benefits are the same.

You can start drinking it the traditional way once you are convinced that it is a healthy and extravagant drink that I could not pass up. You have nothing to lose. In fact you have a lot to gain.

Follow these steps:
1- Pour yerba into your gourd, filling it no more than three quarters full.

2- Cover the mouth of the gourd with the palm of your hand, turn it upside down and shake it several times.

3- Moisten the yerba on one side of the gourd.

4- Once the yerba has absorbed the water, insert the sipping tube into the moistened part.

5- Now start pouring water into your gourd and enjoy all the taste of your unique yerba.

Basic types of Yerba Mate

True mate lovers prefer the gourd for the better and more natural taste and because ,you actually can get more nutrients (vitamins and minerals necessary to sustain life) from the herb with the traditional way, using a straw or bombilla, than the ones you would obtain from tea bags (or pills) for example, as twigs must be removed from the herb to be put in a tea bag.

Yerba Mate with stems (con palos). This is the most popular form of yerba mate in Argentina and Paraguay, and includes both ground yerba leaves and chopped stems that connect the leaves to the branches.

**Yerba Mate without stems (sin palos). This is the most popular form of yerba mate in Uruguay and Brazil, and includes only ground yerba leaves; the stems are removed. For instructions on how to make the perfect brew, read our guide how to prepare yerba mate.**

Yerba Mate is also naturally bitter so you can use Natural Honey or Algave to taste.
