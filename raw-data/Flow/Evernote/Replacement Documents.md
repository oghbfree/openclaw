Job Title: Account Co-ordinator
Reports to: Director of Operations
Responsible for: Contract & temporary staff
Contract: Permanent (Full-time)
Salary: c.£24,000 pa
Job Purpose

To be responsible for the maintaining good relationships with, and developing business from, key accounts in the Institute of Administrative Management (IAM) and the Institute of Professional Administrators (IPA).

Key responsibilities/Accountabilities

As part of the Operations team and working closely with the Business Development Manager and the Membership Manager, the Account Co-ordinator is responsible for:

• Ensuring the process for the accreditation of IAM centres is completed to deadline by all other departments

• Acting as the first contact point for all IAM Accredited Centre queries and ensuring their resolution and recording by other departments

• Building relationships with key contacts in IAM accredited centres through monthly communication and periodic site visits

• Monitoring the use of the IAM name, devices and course details by IAM accredited centres

• Providing advice and assistance to IAM accredited centres on marketing and promotional issues that will increase student numbers

• Making general quality inspection and exam invigilation inspection visits at   IAM accredited centres as required by the IAM Director of Education

• Monitoring IAM accredited centre student performance statistics as required by the IAM Director of Education

• Liaising with Business Development and Marketing & Communications in planning for future sales and marketing campaigns

• Acting as the first contact point for IAM & IPA Corporate Membership activities and  queries and ensuring their resolution and recording by other departments

• Assisting the Membership Manager and Business Development Manager on refining and pitching the IAM and IPA corporate membership offer for specific organisations

• Monitoring management accounts to ensure delivery of financial targets on time and to budget

Person Specification

This is a key role requiring enthusiasm and commitment from a customer-oriented individual who is also a ‘completer-finisher’ capable of seeing tasks through to conclusion.  The position demands a high level of attention to detail and excellent interpersonal and relationship-building skills.  The CfA encourages all individuals to exploit their abilities to the fullest extent and makes significant investment in all employees to support their ongoing career development with professional qualifications.

Essential Skills/Knowledge
• Excellent IT skills
• Strength with internet browser programmes
• Excellent verbal and written communication skills
• Highly developed organisational skills
• Excellent interpersonal and relationship-building skills
• High attention to detail
• Ability to prioritise
• Receptive to innovation and change
• Excellent customer service skills
• Sales skills
Desirable Skills/Knowledge

• A recognised Business & Administration or Business Studies qualification/Maths & English GCSE

• CRM experience
• Account management experience
• Knowledge of the UK education system
