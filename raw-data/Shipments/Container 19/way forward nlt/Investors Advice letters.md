Hi Oman

I’m sure like many of our conscientious investors you have been reading, listening, talking about and absorbing everything to do with property in this time of uncertainty.

We’ve been reading blogs, newspaper articles, attended networking events (virtually of course), listened to Podcasts, weighed up training opportunities, read lots of opinions, looked at education packages and generally been engulfed in what can only be described as NOISE!

It has been so important to us not to be sucked in by the social media chaos and to maintain a cool head and consistent approach when working with investors and analyzing deals.



The best advice we can offer at this time is so simple -

· Don’t make any rash decisions - don’t just plough on with a deal because it stacked in pre-covid times. Take your time, carry out a full risk assessment to take into account all the variables and renegotiate if the deal isn’t what it was previously. Patience is crucial.

· Do your Due Diligence better than you’ve ever done – it’s obvious that the markets are not working in our favour and it is therefore more important than ever that a thorough due diligence process is applied. Make sure the deal stacks when stress-tested from every angle – from finance to rental demand and refurb timescales.

· Look after your business by tightening up on every part of it. Plan everything, leave nothing to chance and make your own decisions about what is right for you and your business.

Its too easy to get blinded by the vast amount of information out there, particularly at this time when so much seems negative. Stay calm, focused and above all don't rush into anything.

Stay positive!

Best Wishes



Hi Oman,

Another week has passed by and we’ve been quizzing mortgage brokers to find out what the word is on the street about the availability of different mortgage products.

Q Will I still be able to get a mortgage product for a HMO?

A Yes there are products, but the range has been massively reduced with many well-known lenders totally withdrawing products from the market. However, there are products still available, but providers will be risk assessing at a far higher level than usual. Due to this they will be offering products with a lower LTV and higher rates of interest. Even more stringent security checks will be carried out on limited companies and/ or directors and sole traders. We have spoken to several mortgage brokers who have all said that they still welcome enquiries and each that one will be considered on a case by case basis.

Q How long can you see this situation lasting

A The experienced brokers we spoke to are already making comparisons to the 2008 downturn and they are predicting that the current lending conditions will last for 12-18 months.

Q Is there any good news?

A We have heard it again Cash is King. If you are an investor with an excellent credit score or an existing landlord (ideally portfolio), again with an outstanding credit score and security ie cash, there will be lenders willing to lend or refinance.

Q Is commercial lending still an option?

A All brokers are aware that lenders were stung in 2008 and immediately looked to risk assess their exposure, this is now happening again and HMOs up to 5 bed are likely to only be valued at their bricks and mortar value. They would also be surprised that even larger HMOS custom built for the job would achieve lending on an income multiplier. However, an HMO that can show 2+years of rental income through ASTs will be in a stronger situation that newly converted HMOs with no prior trading history.

Q Is it likely that property will be down-valued?

A The whole conveyancing process has almost come to a standstill due to social distancing and no viewings taking place. Furthermore, the legal exchange and completion are being thwarted as solicitors still generally refuse to accept electronic signatures. Valuers that are getting out to properties are already down valuing properties – both residential and commercial.

****That all sounds pretty negative and the news that investors and vendors are exposed to on a daily basis****

Vendors are already reacting to this news and an entrusted estate agent in our goldmine patch has already stated that the market changed from a vendors market to an investors market almost overnight - the expression ‘When people are greedy get scared and when people are scared get greedy’ has never been truer. Not only are vendors being far more flexible with their price, but they are also becoming open to conditional sales.

More of this to come.
